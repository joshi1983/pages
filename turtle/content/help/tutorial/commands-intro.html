The most common drawing commands are:

  • fd or forward
  • bk, back, or backward
  • rt, or right
  • lt or left
  • pr or print

bk, back, or backward represents the same command. Each of the above commands requires one number as input. The number adds more information to what the command should do

  • fd 100 or forward 100 means to "move the turtle forward 100 units".
  • bk 100 or back 100 or backward 100 mean to "move the turtle backward 100 units".
  • rt 45 or right 45 means to "turn the turtle right 45 degrees".
  • Try to guess what lt or left does with its input.

The graphics screen has a coordinate system. If you move your cursor around the graphics screen, you'll see the coordinates of the cursor show in the lower right corner. The turtle is initially at 0, 0 and heads up the y-axis to increased y-coordinates. X increases as you look toward the right side of the graphics screen.

Try it for yourself!

Type forward 100 into the text field at the bottom of the web page and click the "Run" button to see the turtle move forward 100 units.

Here are some more examples. Try with different numbers and see how that affects how the turtle moves and rotates.

  1. forward 100 right 120 forward 100 right 120 forward 100
  2. forward 100 right 90 forward 100 right 90 forward 100 right 90 forward 100

Those will run extremely fast. Slow down how fast those commands execute and run them again. In the top menu, click Debugging -> Maximum Speed and replace with "Very Slow". Watching the code execute slowly sometimes helps you understand it better.
